Lisbon, Portugal

Lisbon, the vibrant capital of Portugal, is one of Europe’s oldest cities, founded by the Phoenicians around 1200 BC. During the 15th and 16th centuries, Portugal rose as a global power thanks to the Age of Discovery, led by navigators like Vasco da Gama, who pioneered the sea route to India, and Pedro Álvares Cabral, who reached Brazil. As a strategic hub, Lisbon was the heart of these expeditions, driven by the innovations of the Sagres School and breakthroughs in navigation. The city still bears landmarks from this golden era, such as the Belém Tower and the Jerónimos Monastery. Safe and sun-drenched, Lisbon is a treasure blending heroic history with contemporary charm.
